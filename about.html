DESIGNED MIND 2024

How does subjective experience evolve and what new capabilities does it enable? How easy will it be (by design or by accident) to build conscious machines?

Modern scientific theories of consciousness (proposed by the likes of Dennett, Meztinger and Graziano) construe subjectivity as a particularly rich form of self-simulation. While self-control may always involve some form of self-representation, conscious systems seem to take this further: by representing itself as a situated agent with all kinds of opportunity for action, both overt and covert, as its disposal, this self-simulation or self-model permits new levels of autonomy, self-monitoring and deliberation. On this view subjectivity evolves hand-in-hand with the kind of deliberative, hyper-plastic behaviour typical of humans.

This emerging understanding of consciousness as key to unlocking powerful new capabilities is now seeing practical application in the next generation of generative AI. Although recent advances have been impressive, current systems still fall far short of human-style reasoning, and some researchers are looking to theories of subjectivity as inspiration for new machine architectures. If our theories of consciousness are right then the prospect of building a machine that attributes to itself a rich inner mental life, as humans do, is becoming a plausible reality. While the ethical and societal implications of doing so are vast, its scientific and philosophical significance is also not to be underestimated.

This two-day symposium will bring together cognitive scientists, philosophers, computer scientists, roboticists, neuroscientists and biologists interested in the science, evolution and engineering of subjectivity and deliberative action.
